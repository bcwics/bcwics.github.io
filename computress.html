Why Attend?

Learn

Hakcathons are known to be valuable learning experiences - and Computress is no different. Learn a new technical skill while you hack, and learn how to work in a team under a time constraint!

Build

Create something! An app, a website, an Alexa skill - the possibilities are endless.

Expand

Meet a diverse new group of people to work with and befriend. Grow your network and understand other peoples' perspectives and experiences.

FAQ

What is Computress?

The Brooklyn College Women in Computer Science club is hosting its first ever all-women hackathon! Come to Computress and bring a friend for a free, coding competition jam packed with activities, food, and the chance to meet other women in tech.

Is Computress free to attend?

Yes it is! The event includes admission, food, and a lot of free gear and swag!



Who can come?

Any and all above 18 female and non-binary identified students and professionals.

Do I need coding experience?

Nope. Just bring yourself and a computer! There will lots of opportunities to learn at the hackathon.



Who’s organizing this whole thing anyways?

The Brooklyn College Women in Computer Science Club. For more information, you can visit bcwics.github.io or contact bcwomenincompsci@gmail.com!

Can I volunteer/mentor?

Of course! Please contact bcwomenincompsci@gmail.com with details on how you’re interested in assisting.



I represent a company, how can we help?

There are many ways you can help, including, but not limited to, sponsorship! Please refer to the following document or contact bcwomenincompsci@gmail.com

What should I bring?

You must bring a valid student or government issued ID card for admission. Bring your laptop and charging cables and any other specific materials you’ll need for your hacking project. Everything else will be provided!
